## Problem Statement

Syval exists as a working prototype (gtm-brain) with a PRD, interactive demo, agent path, and a first customer prospect (Maruthi at Nevara). The question is: how does this become a sustainable business — not just a tool that gets you a job?

## Demand Evidence

- Maruthi (Nevara) explicitly listed 13 growth ideas that map to Syval's three tools
- People asked for Pingi access after seeing it get the founder blocked by Paula Graham on X — pull-based demand from visible results
- The founder knows specific people beyond Maruthi who would use this
- The GTM tool market is $4.1B (2025) heading to $15B by 2030 — the space is hot

## Status Quo

Solo founders and early GTM hires currently stitch together:
- Sales Navigator ($99/mo) for lead lists
- Dripify ($99/mo) for automated sequences
- Clay ($149+/mo) for data enrichment
- Instantly ($30/mo) for email outreach
- Manual LinkedIn scrolling (4+ hrs/week)
- No learning system connecting any of it

Total: $300-500/mo + 16+ hours/month of manual work. No data carries over between tools. Every campaign starts from scratch.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary:** First GTM hire at a seed-stage startup. No budget for 4 separate tools. Needs to prove outbound works in 2 weeks or the founder stops investing in the channel.

**Wedge:** LinkedIn lead scraping + AI DM drafting. This is what Maruthi asked for first. It's live, it works, it delivers value on day 1.

**Expansion:** X engagement → content creation → brain insights → autonomous agent. Each layer adds retention and increases willingness to pay.

## Premises

1. Syval is a product company, funded by a job at Nevara. IP stays separate from Nevara.
2. The moat is accumulated market-specific data, not the tool itself. Cold start is the biggest risk.
3. Pricing: $99/mo at launch, $149/mo once brain insights are proven. Replaces $300-500/mo tool stack.
4. First 5 customers come from personal network (people who saw Pingi, founders the founder knows).
5. The product must deliver value on DAY 1 (scraping + DM drafting) before the brain has data. Brain is the retention play, not the acquisition play.
6. Path: Maruthi proves it → 5 more users → brain starts working → raise or grow organically.

## Business Model

### Pricing

| Tier | Price | What you get |
|------|-------|-------------|
| **Starter** | Free | 5 scrapes/month, 10 DM drafts, no brain insights, no X engage |
| **Pro** | $99/mo | Unlimited scrapes, unlimited DMs, X engagement, content generation, weekly brain insights |
| **Pro+** | $149/mo | Everything in Pro + daily brain insights, auto-draft queue (L2 copilot mode), priority support |

Free tier exists for acquisition — let people try the scraping flow without commitment. The ICP match rate + DM draft quality is the "aha moment" that converts to Pro.

### Cold start solution

The brain is empty on day 1. Here's how to make the product valuable anyway:

**Day 1 value (no data needed):**
- Paste a LinkedIn post → get 500+ leads in 30 seconds (vs 4 hours manual)
- ICP filter → 50+ qualified leads instantly
- AI drafts a personalized DM for each lead (vs copy-paste templates)
- Sales Nav CSV export (vs manual list building)
- X tweet surfacing + AI reply drafts

**Week 1 value (first data):**
- "You've scraped 8 posts. Here are your top topics by ICP match rate." (first brain signal)
- DM template that references comments (already better than generic)

**Month 1 value (brain awakens):**
- "Engineering hiring posts yield 3x more ICP leads for your market"
- "Comment-reference DMs get 23% reply rate vs 8% generic"
- Weekly report with specific recommendations

**Month 3 value (full brain):**
- System tells you what to post, who to DM, when to scrape
- Inbound vs outbound comparison
- ICP refinement suggestions
- Full attribution: content → lead → DM → reply → meeting

The day-1 value is the TOOL. The month-3 value is the BRAIN. Users sign up for the tool and stay for the brain.

## Go-to-Market

### Phase 1: Warm network (Month 1-2)
- Pitch Maruthi → prove it works for Nevara
- Onboard 4 more people from your network (people who saw Pingi, founders you know)
- No marketing, no landing page updates, no paid ads
- Goal: 5 users generating data, brain starts learning

### Phase 2: Content-led growth (Month 3-4)
- Post about what you're learning from the brain data (anonymized insights)
- "We analyzed 500 LinkedIn outbound DMs. Here's what actually works."
- Share specific numbers: reply rates by DM angle, ICP match rates by topic
- The brain's insights become your marketing content
- Goal: 10-20 inbound signups from content

### Phase 3: Product-led growth (Month 5-6)
- Free tier drives signups
- The "aha moment" (paste URL → see qualified leads in 30 seconds) converts free → paid
- Brain insights visible as a locked feature ("Upgrade to Pro to see which topics work")
- Goal: 50 users, word-of-mouth growing

### Phase 4: Consider fundraising (Month 9-12)
- 100 users, $10K MRR
- Clear data on: retention (do users with brain data churn less?), unit economics, growth rate
- Pitch: "We've proven that accumulated GTM data is a moat. Here's the data."
- Or: don't raise. $10K MRR as a solo founder is a great business.

## Competitive Positioning

### One-line positioning
"Syval is the GTM tool that learns your market. Clay gives you data. Syval gives you decisions."

### Why not Clay/Apollo/Instantly?

| Competitor | What they do well | What they miss |
|-----------|------------------|---------------|
| Clay | Data enrichment (150+ providers) | Doesn't learn what works for YOUR market |
| Apollo | Huge contact database | Stale data, no engagement-based discovery |
| Instantly | Email at scale | Email only, no LinkedIn, no learning |
| Lemlist | Multi-channel sequences | No brain, no content, no X engage |

### Syval's wedge
**Engagement-based lead discovery** — not "who matches a profile" but "who is actively engaging with content in your space RIGHT NOW." These leads are warmer because they're already thinking about the topic. No other tool does this.

## Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-----------|
| LinkedIn blocks Apify | Scraping stops working | LinkedIn API application pending. Multiple actor fallbacks. |
| Brain takes too long to be useful | Users churn before month 3 | Day-1 tool value is strong enough to retain without brain |
| Maruthi doesn't convert | No proof point, no first customer | Other people in network. Brain works for any ICP, not just Nevara. |
| IP conflict with Nevara | Maruthi claims ownership of Syval | Keep code separate. Be explicit in the pitch: "I bring my own tools." |
| Funded competitors add learning | Clay adds a brain feature | Your 6 months of market-specific data can't be replicated. Switching cost is the moat. |

## The Assignment

Before building the backend, do two things:

1. **Have the IP conversation with Maruthi.** Before you pitch, say: "I've built GTM tools that I'll use to run your outbound. The tools are mine — I bring them as a perk of hiring me, not as something I build for Nevara. Is that clear?" Get a verbal yes. If he pushes back, you need to know now.

2. **Message 3 other potential users.** Not a pitch — a question: "I built a tool that scrapes LinkedIn post engagers and drafts personalized DMs. Want to try it?" If 2 out of 3 say yes, you have a business. If 0, you have a tool for Maruthi.
