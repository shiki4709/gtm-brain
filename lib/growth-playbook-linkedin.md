# LinkedIn Growth Playbook — GTM Brain

Used by the recommendation engine and goal suggestion system to help users grow on LinkedIn.

## Algorithm in 2025-2026 (360Brew AI Model)
- LinkedIn deployed "360Brew," a 150B-parameter AI model that reshapes distribution
- Personal profiles get 2.75x more impressions and 5x more engagement vs company pages
- Saves/bookmarks = 5x stronger signal than likes (create save-worthy content)
- Meaningful comments (>15 words) weighted 12-15x a like
- Dwell time is #1 signal — long posts that keep people reading win
- First 60-90 minutes determine reach ("golden hour")
- 360Brew performs "profile content audition" — cross-references posts against headline, About, experience
- AI-generated content detected and deprioritized by 360Brew
- Accounts under 10K followers see engagement 369% higher than average

## Algorithm Punishments
- External links: 60% reach penalty (put links in first comment)
- Profile-content mismatch (posting off-topic from headline/experience)
- AI-generated template content (360Brew detects it)
- Engagement pods (detected and penalized)
- Hashtag overuse (triggers spam detection, max 3-5)
- Editing posts after publishing (reportedly reduces distribution)
- Text-only posts getting weaker (0.88x multiplier, shrinking 28.4%)

## Post Formats Ranked by Reach (Socialinsider 2026 benchmarks)
1. Carousels/Documents — 4.0-4.1x vs text, 6.60% engagement rate, 15-20 sec dwell time
2. Polls — 1.64x reach (regained strength in 2025-2026)
3. Native video — 1.2-1.4x, growing in importance
4. Image + text — 1.0-1.2x, solid baseline
5. Text-only — 0.88x, weakest format, median reach shrank 28.4%
6. External links — penalized 60%

## Hook Formulas (first 2 lines before "see more")
~210 characters shown before fold. Everything depends on these lines.

1. Bold number: "I said no to a $500K deal." / "87% of managers never received training."
2. Vulnerable admission: "I almost quit last month." / "I've been faking it for 3 years."
3. Contrarian claim: "Stop networking. It's a waste of time."
4. Curiosity gap: "The worst advice I ever got made me a millionaire."
5. List tease: "5 things I wish I knew before raising my Series A:"
6. Before/after: "6 months ago I had [bad state]. Today I have [good state]."
7. Question: "Why do 90% of [audience] still [common mistake]?"

Rules: Line 1 = pattern interrupt, Line 2 = curiosity gap, no hashtags or emojis in hook.

## Comment Strategy — #1 Growth Lever
76% of accounts that grew from sub-5K to 25K+ in Q1 2026 used strategic commenting as primary tactic.

### Execution
1. Build list of 50 accounts — thought leaders, potential clients, peers
2. Turn on post notifications for top 20
3. Comment within first 60-90 minutes of their posts (early comments get locked at top)
4. Write comments >15 words that add insight, data, or genuine question
5. Target 10-15 meaningful comments per day
6. Spend 30-45 minutes each morning on this before posting own content
7. Expected: 50-100 profile visits/day, 200-500 new followers/month

### Comment Formulas
- Agree + Add: "This matches what I've seen. When we [specific thing], the result was [outcome]."
- Respectful challenge: "I'd push back on [point] because in [context], we found [data]."
- Thread starter: "Curious — did you find [variable] changed the outcome?"

## Posting Frequency (Buffer, 2M+ posts analyzed)
- 2-5 posts/week: +1,182 impressions per post, +0.23% engagement rate
- Optimal for solo founder: 3-5 posts/week
- More than 2 posts/day cannibalize each other's reach

## Best Times to Post (4.8M posts analyzed, 2026)
- Best days: Tuesday through Thursday (Tuesday highest)
- Best times: 3-8 PM weekdays; 4 PM Wednesday + 3-4 PM Friday = peak
- Sleeper time: Sunday (lower competition)
- First 60-90 minutes are critical — be online to respond

## Daily Targets by Growth Stage

### 0-500 followers (Month 1-2) — Starter
- 3 posts/week (1 carousel, 1 story, 1 tactical)
- 10-15 comments/day on bigger accounts
- 20-30 targeted connection requests/day (with personalized notes)
- Optimize profile: benefit-driven headline, keyword-rich About
- Weekly goal: 3 posts, 50-75 comments, 20 connections

### 500-2,000 followers (Month 2-4) — Growing
- 4-5 posts/week (2 carousels, 2 text, 1 video/poll)
- 15 comments/day
- Start batch creating (10-15 posts in one sitting)
- Weekly goal: 5 posts, 75-100 comments, 1-2 carousels

### 2,000-10K followers (Month 4-12) — Scaling
- 5 posts/week + LinkedIn newsletter
- 15-20 comments/day
- Add native video (60-second clips)
- Collaborate with other creators
- Weekly goal: 5 posts, 100+ comments, 1 newsletter, 1 video

## LinkedIn-Specific Growth Hacks
1. Comment before posting: 15 min engaging first, algorithm sees you as "active"
2. Reply to every comment within 60 min (golden hour for distribution)
3. Profile banner = CTA (promote newsletter, product, free resource)
4. Creator mode: turns "Connect" to "Follow" (lower friction)
5. Featured section: pin best posts, newsletter, media
6. Cross-post X threads as LinkedIn carousels (wait 24-48 hours between platforms)
7. Links in first comment, NEVER in post body
8. Tag sparingly: max 3 tags, only people who will engage

## Carousel/Document Best Practices
- Highest-performing format at 4.0-4.1x reach
- 8-12 slides optimal
- Hook on slide 1, one insight per slide, CTA on last slide
- Use Canva or repurpose X threads as carousels
- Frameworks, step-by-step guides, and "steal my playbook" perform best

## Common Mistakes
1. AI-generated posts — 360Brew detects, reach craters
2. Links in post body — 60% penalty
3. Profile-content mismatch — algorithm suppresses off-topic
4. Text-only reliance — 0.88x and shrinking
5. Inconsistent posting — 3/week for 6 months beats 10/week for 2 weeks
6. Ignoring golden hour — first 60-90 min = distribution window
7. Generic comments — "Great post!" = zero growth value
8. Chasing vanity metrics — optimize for saves, comments, profile visits
9. Posting from company page — fraction of personal profile reach
10. Posting on weekends — 40-60% less reach (unless global audience)
